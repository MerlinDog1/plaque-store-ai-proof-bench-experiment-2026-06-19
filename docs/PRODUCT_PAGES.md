### Product Description

Bench plaques are compact, durable engraved plates designed for timber benches, memorial seats, garden furniture, and outdoor features. The shallow format works best with short, clear wording: a name, date, dedication, donor line, or short message.

The proof bench shows the plaque at its real proportions, so you can see how much space the wording has before approval. If the text is too long for a compact bench plaque, the site should recommend a larger size, shorter wording, or human artwork review.

### Best For

- Memorial benches
- Donor and dedication plaques
- Garden seats
- Park and community benches
- Timber rails and outdoor features

### Recommended Defaults

Material:

Brushed stainless for a clean outdoor look, brushed brass for a traditional engraved finish, aged brass for a warmer heritage feel.

Size:

150 x 50mm as the compact starting point. Offer larger custom widths for longer wording.

Fixings:

Countersunk screws or flat circular caps, depending on the desired finish.

Border:

Simple border only where the plaque has enough height. Avoid scalloped styles on bench-format plaques.

Wood backing:

Optional. Useful when the plaque needs more presence or a clean mounting surface.

### Fit Guidance

Bench plaques are intentionally shallow. Short wording looks best. If the proof warns that the layout is tight, choose a longer plaque, reduce the wording, remove the border, or request a human check.

### Proof Empty State

### Approval Prompt

Check the wording carefully. Bench plaques have limited height, so spelling, dates, line breaks, and text size should all be reviewed before approval.

## Material Guide Copy

### Guide Intro

Choose the material first. It affects the look, durability, text contrast, and price of the plaque. The proof bench updates the plaque preview and size pricing as soon as the material changes.

### Brushed Brass

Brushed brass is the classic engraved plaque finish. It has a warm gold tone with a fine horizontal satin grain, giving the plaque a traditional look without the high glare of polished metal.

Best for:

- Wall plaques
- Bench plaques
- Dedication plaques
- Opening plaques
- Traditional business plaques

Proof note:

Dark filled engraving gives strong contrast on brushed brass. Cream or light text can be used for certain lacquered looks, but standard dark text is the safest default.

### Polished Brass

Polished brass has a brighter, more reflective gold finish. It feels more formal and decorative than brushed brass, making it suitable for presentation plaques and traditional interiors.

Best for:

- Formal wall plaques
- Award and presentation plaques
- Heritage or ceremonial wording
- Interior business plaques

Proof note:

Reflections can make polished brass look different in photographs and on screen. Use the proof for layout and wording, and the material guide for finish expectations.

### Aged Brass

Aged brass has a warmer, darker, more subdued brass appearance. It suits heritage, garden, and traditional settings where a bright polished finish would feel too new.

Best for:

- Garden plaques
- Tree dedications
- Heritage-style wall plaques
- Traditional bench plaques

Proof note:

The darker base tone gives a softer, more established look. Keep wording clear and avoid overly dense layouts.

### Orbital Brass With Matt Lacquer

Orbital brass with matt lacquer has a muted brass tone with a fine orbital surface pattern under a low-glare finish. It is useful when the customer wants brass warmth without a mirror-like shine.

Best for:

- Modern brass plaques
- Interior wall plaques
- Presentation plaques
- Designs needing softer glare control

Proof note:

Text colour choices matter on this finish. The proof should show the selected text colour clearly.

### Brushed Stainless

Brushed stainless has a cool silver tone with a fine satin grain. It gives a modern, practical look and is a strong choice for business, exterior, garden, and contemporary plaques.

Best for:

- Business plaques
- Outdoor signs
- Garden plaques
- Modern wall plaques
- Bench plaques

Proof note:

Dark engraving reads cleanly on brushed stainless. It is a good default when the setting is modern or exposed.

### Mirror Stainless

Mirror stainless is highly reflective and has a bright contemporary appearance. It works best where the customer wants a polished modern finish and understands that reflections are part of the look.

Best for:

- Modern interior plaques
- Premium business plaques
- Presentation plaques
- Contemporary signage

Proof note:

The proof shows layout and contrast, but real mirror stainless reflects its surroundings. Use concise wording and strong contrast.

## Shared FAQ For Product Pages

### Which plaque type should I choose?

Choose bench for shallow timber-mounted plaques, wall for most general engraved plaques, tree and garden for outdoor markers, business for professional signs, and bespoke for custom sizes or unusual requirements.

### Can I approve the AI proof straight away?

Yes, if the wording, layout, material, size, fixings, border, backing, and price are correct. If anything feels uncertain, request a human artwork check.

### What happens after approval?

The approved proof and selected options become the production reference. The plaque is then made to order and delivered in days.

### Can I get help choosing a size?

Yes. The proof bench should flag tight wording and recommend a larger plaque when needed. Bespoke and long-wording orders can be sent for human review.

### Why does a border affect the proof?

A border uses some of the plaque face and reduces the safe area available for text. On small plaques, removing the border can make the wording clearer.

### Why do fixings affect the layout?

Screws and caps sit near the plaque edges. The proof keeps important text clear of those positions.

### Is the proof colour exact?

No screen can show metal finish perfectly. The proof is for wording, layout, relative scale, borders, fixings, and chosen material style. Real metal and wood finishes may vary slightly.

### Can I use very long wording?

Yes, but long wording needs enough plaque area. If the proof is crowded, choose a larger size or request a human check.
