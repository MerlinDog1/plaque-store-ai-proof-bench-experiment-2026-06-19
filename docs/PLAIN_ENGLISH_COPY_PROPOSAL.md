# InstaPlaque plain-English copy proposal

This is a copy draft, not an implementation. It deliberately keeps the pages short. Search terms belong in titles and headings where they are useful; they should not make the customer read the same point repeatedly.

## Rules for every page

- Say what the customer can buy.
- Explain the proof in one sentence.
- Show the useful starting price and what is included.
- Mention only choices that help someone order.
- Use short headings, short paragraphs and ordinary words.
- Do not explain plaque-making jargon unless the customer must make that choice.
- Do not repeat the same sales claim in several sections.

### The only guidance most customers need

**Keep the wording short**  
A name, dates and one short message usually works best on a 150 × 50 mm plaque. The online proof will show you if it feels crowded.

**Choose the finish**  
Brass has a traditional gold appearance. Stainless steel has a cleaner silver appearance.

**Check the bench first**  
If the bench belongs to a council, cemetery or club, ask them what size and fixings they allow before ordering.

## Copy to remove or avoid

- “AI typesetter”, “production-style layout” and “engraving-safe area” in sales copy.
- Long explanations of borders, margins and fixing positions before the customer enters the designer.
- Separate sections that restate the same proof-before-payment promise.
- Lists of search phrases presented as customer content.
- Emotional clichés such as “timeless tribute”.
